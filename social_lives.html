Social Lives

/ so what's the situation

With media like instagram, snapchat, twitter, facebook, and more, connecting people doesn’t seem to be a problem.
Or that’s what you would like to think. In reality, connecting with people has also been a way to get more likes and trying to get as many followers as possible. However, when times get rough you need to know who’s by your side and who’s not. So, how do you navigate through your social life?

/ Tip One

Making friends is a skill that can be learned.
Good therapy says Rather than just looking around for someone with common interests, helping teens become clear about who they are and what they value allows them to attract friends who will be a good fit for them.

Besides, talk to people face to face, don't always be on social media. Take a break from social media every so often, and look at what's happening around you. Talk to someone new. Just live life!

/ Tip two

Friendships change with the times, so go with the flow.
It’s alright if someone that was close to you when you were younger drifts away. Take the situation as an opportunity to meet new people with similar interests. As you get more interests and activities, friends that can relate better will come to you and friends can't relate as much will drift. Simple as that. As the times change, your social life will change.

/ Tip three

Not every acquaintance will be a bff.
Some people are going to be in your life forever. While others, not so much. So don't get hung up if someone you talk to a lot isn't your closest friend. Life is full of surprises and choices so relax and don't get hungup if something doesn't go to plan. After all, each ending is a new beginning.

/ Tip four

Conflicts are a part of relationships.
Conflicts are natural in every part of life. In any relationship, fights are bound to happen, so understand that an argument does not mean the end of a relationship.
